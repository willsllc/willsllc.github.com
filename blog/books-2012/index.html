Most Thought-Provoking Books of 2012

  1. The Idea Factory: Bell Labs and the Great Age of American Innovation
    by Jon Gertner published March 15, 2012
    Over the span of a few decades, a single research lab invented the transistor, the microprocessor, radar, the communication satellite, the CD, and more.
  2. The Power of Habit: Why We Do What We Do in Life and Business
    3. by Charles Duhigg published February 28, 2012
    Why toothpaste tingles, how Febreeze was a flop, and hundreds of other tidbits that are perfect for cocktail parties and future Jeopardy episodes.
  3. The Signal and the Noise: Why So Many Predictions Fail-but Some Don't
    by Nate Silver published September 27, 2012
    Weaves together baseball, earthquakes, the weather, poker, and terrorism. Chapter 7 is the best description of Bayes theorem I've ever read.
  4. The Honest Truth About Dishonesty: How We Lie to Everyone---Especially Ourselves
    by Dan Ariely published June 5, 2012
    The third Ariely book, and just as fun. Would be ranked #1 except it's essentially the same formula as his prior two gems.
  5. Automate This: How Algorithms Came to Rule Our World
    by Christopher Steiner published August 30, 2012
    Surprisingly good read from a first-time author (and YC alum). Expands on Andreessen's quip to cover trading, counter-terrorism, the Arab Spring and more.
